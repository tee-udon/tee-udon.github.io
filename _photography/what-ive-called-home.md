> 那些消逝了的岁月，仿佛隔着一块积着灰尘的玻璃，看得到，抓不着。
>
> He remembers those vanished years. As though looking through a dusty window pane, the past is something he could see, but not touch.
>
> <cite>— 王家卫《花样年华》/ Wong Kar-wai, In the Mood for Love (2000)</cite>

Michael and Taylor "go to church" together after work — their daily ritual of reading and writing in silence. While I was visiting them in DC last April, they let me join. A week isn't long, and we hadn't known each other long either. But when I got back to my apartment in Palo Alto, I missed the silence, the backyard, and them. It was the first time I understood that I could be homesick for somewhere I didn't live.

If home can arrive in a week, it can leave in one too. People drift. Places fill with new faces. Old memories get buried beneath newer ones. Lately I've been trying to pay better attention. I linger a little longer. I try to notice the small, ordinary things before they become things I miss. And I reach for my camera more often than I used to. 

A photograph won't keep a home from changing. But it keeps something of it alive.
