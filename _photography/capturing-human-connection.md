> "Isn't everything we do in life a way to be loved a little more?"
>
> <cite>— Celine, Before Sunrise</cite>

I used to live anywhere but the present. My mind was either stuck in the past, dissecting old mistakes, or racing toward an uncertain future, worrying about my experiments or student loans. These thoughts were a constant background noise that made it hard to find joy.

That changed when I started walking San Francisco.

From the slopes of Mission Dolores to the shores of Ocean Beach, I tried to capture the city's beauty with my camera. But when I reviewed my photos, I noticed something surprising: it wasn't the landscapes that moved me, but the people inhabiting them. The city's hills and streets became conduits for human connection.

I view life as an integral—a sum of infinite, small moments. We often worry so much about the "area under the curve" that we miss the beauty of each small slice itself. Photography forces me to slow down, listen to my surroundings, and capture the fleeting, mundane, and beautiful evidence of us connecting with one another.
